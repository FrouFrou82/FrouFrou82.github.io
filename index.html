About Me

Life is messy, and so are the systems we rely on. I’ve learned as much from juggling family schedules, school forms, and household workflows as I have from working across energy, government, and infrastructure. I notice patterns, cut duplication, and fold insights into strategy — helping services and processes actually work for the people using them. My approach blends user-centred thinking, systems thinking, agile principles and a fair splash of idisyncracy for flavour.
